About

I'm Sasha. I study how large language models fail — hallucinations, overconfident answers, and the gap between fluent text and grounded truth.

My work sits at the intersection of uncertainty quantification, mechanistic interpretability, and evaluation. I'm especially interested in methods that are practical enough to deploy: lightweight signals you can compute at inference time without retraining the model.

Research interests

  • Hallucination detection and calibration in LLMs
  • Attention sinks, induction heads, and internal model dynamics
  • Uncertainty estimation for retrieval-augmented generation
  • Benchmarking and reproducible evaluation pipelines

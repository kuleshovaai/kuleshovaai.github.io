Induction-aware entropy gating

June 2026

Semantic entropy is a strong black-box signal for hallucination detection: sample multiple completions, cluster by meaning, and measure disagreement. But in RAG settings, the signal gets noisy when the model leans on parametric knowledge instead of retrieved context.

The idea

Attention sinks — tokens that consistently attract disproportionate attention — correlate with certain failure modes. INTRYGUE gates entropy estimates using sink-rate dynamics, down-weighting uncertainty scores when the model appears to be in an "induction-heavy" regime that's prone to confabulation.

Open questions

  • How stable is sink-rate across model families and scales?
  • Does gating help on out-of-distribution retrieval, or only in-domain?
  • What's the latency overhead at inference time?
